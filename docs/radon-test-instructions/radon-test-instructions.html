Running the Test
1
Wait at least 48 hours after the system has been running before you start testing.
2
Using the test kit provided, place the vial(s):
  • At least 3 feet from doors, walls, and windows
  • At least 2 feet off the floor
  • At least 4 inches from other objects
  • In an area that stays at or above 55°F for the entire test
If using 2 vials, place them 4 inches apart in the same location.
3
Remove the cap from the vial(s). At the same time, begin filling in the laboratory information sheet found in the same box.
Important: The testing period must run 48–96 hours — no more, no less.
4
After the testing period, replace the cap on the vial(s) and return them to the pre-posted box with the completed information sheet.
The back page of the information sheet has a spot for vial numbers. Write down your vial number(s) and tear that sheet off for your records.
5
Mail the kit back promptly once testing has ended.
Prompt shipping ensures the kit reaches the laboratory within the required time frame. If you’re concerned about timing, feel free to ship by FedEx or UPS — but since the laboratory is in Massachusetts, delivery should not be a problem.
Filling Out the Laboratory Information Sheet
  1. Under “Report To,” fill in your own name and address — not Eagle Environmental, Inc.
  2. Disregard the technician name and certification number.
  3. Start and finish dates and times must be filled in to receive results, along with the number(s) on the side of the vial(s) — the device number.
Temperature requirement: Do not report a temperature below 55°F. A test area recorded below 55°F will be considered invalid by the laboratory.
Don’t forget to place the information sheet in the box!
